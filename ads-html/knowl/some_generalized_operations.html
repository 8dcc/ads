Example1.5.4Some generalized operations

If \(A_1 = \{0, 2, 3\}\), \(A_2 = \{1, 2, 3, 6\}\), and \(A_3 = \{-1, 0, 3, 9\}\), then \[\underset{i=1}{\overset{4}{\cap }}A_i=A_1\cap A_2\cap A_3=\{3\}\] and \[\underset{i=1}{\overset{4}{\cup }}A_i=A_1\cup A_2\cup A_3=\{-1,0,1,2,3,6,9\}\] With this notation it is quite easy to write lengthy expressions in a fairly compact form. For example, the statement \[A\cap \left(B_1\cup B_2\cup \cdots \cup B_n\right)= \left(A\cap B_1\right)\cup \left(A\cap B_2\right)\cup \cdots \cup \left(A\cap B_n\right)\] becomes \[A \cap \left(\underset{i=1}{\overset{n}{\cup }}B_i\right)= \underset{i=1}{\overset{n}{\cup }}\left(A\cap B_n\right)\]
