Theorem 15.3.17 suggests that \(S_n\) can be partitioned into its “even” and “odd” elements. For example, the even permutations of \(S_3\) are \(i\text{,}\) \(r_1=(1,2,3)=(1,3)(1,2)\) and \(r_2=(1,3,2)=(1,2)(1,3)\text{.}\) They form a subgroup, \(\left\{i,r_1,r_2\right\}\) of \(S_3\text{.}\)
