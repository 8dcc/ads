To compute \(\gcd(a,b)\text{,}\) we divide \(b\) into \(a\) and get a remainder \(r\) such that \(0\leq r <\lvert b\rvert \text{.}\) By the property above, \(\gcd(a, b)= \gcd(b, r)\text{.}\) We repeat the process until we get zero for a remainder. The last nonzero number that is the second entry in our pairs is the greatest common divisor. This is inevitable because the second number in each pair is smaller than the previous one.
