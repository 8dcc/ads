The last matrix equation will have nonzero solutions if and only if

\begin{equation*} \det \left( \begin{array}{cc} 2-\lambda & 1 \\ 2 & 3-\lambda \\ \end{array} \right) =0 \end{equation*}

or \((2 - \lambda )(3 -\lambda ) - 2 = 0\text{,}\) which simplifies to \(\lambda^2 - 5\lambda + 4 = 0\text{.}\) Therefore, the solutions to this quadratic equation, \(\lambda_1 = 1\) and \(\lambda_2 = 4\text{,}\) are the eigenvalues of \(A\text{.}\) We now have to find eigenvectors associated with each eigenvalue.
