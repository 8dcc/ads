Item 2.

A recurrence relationship of the form \(S_k = a S_{k-1} + b\text{,}\) where \(a\) and \(b\) are constants, is called a first-order difference equation. In order to write out the sequence, we need to know one initial condition. Equations of this type can be solved similarly to the method outlined in the example by introducing the superfluous equation \(1 =0\cdot F_{k-1}+1\) to obtain in matrix equation:

\begin{equation*} \left( \begin{array}{c} F_k \\ 1 \\ \end{array} \right)=\left( \begin{array}{cc} a & b \\ 0 & 1 \\ \end{array} \right)\left( \begin{array}{c} S_{k-1} \\ 1 \\ \end{array} \right)\textrm{ }\Rightarrow \left( \begin{array}{c} F_k \\ 1 \\ \end{array} \right)=\left( \begin{array}{cc} a & b \\ 0 & 1 \\ \end{array} \right)^k\left( \begin{array}{c} F_0 \\ 1 \\ \end{array} \right) \end{equation*}
