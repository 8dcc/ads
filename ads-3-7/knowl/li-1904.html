Item 11.5.5.6.a.

By its definition, \(\langle S \rangle\) has all of the properties needed to be a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\) The only thing that isn't obvious is that the identity of \(G\) is in \(\langle S \rangle\text{.}\) Prove that the identity of \(G\) is in \(\langle S \rangle\text{.}\)
