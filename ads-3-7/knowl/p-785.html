Let us reconsider the Counting with No Order. There are \(3 ! = 6\) different orderings for each of the three-element subsets. The table below lists each subset of \(A\) and all permutations of each subset on the same line.

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{cc} \textrm{subset} & \textrm{permutations} \\ \{a, b, c\} & abc,acb,bca,bac,cab,cba \\ \{a, b, d\} & abd,adb,bda,bad,dab,dba \\ \{a, c, d\} & acd,adc,cda,cad,dac,dca \\ \{b, c, d\} & bcd,bdc,cdb,cbd,dbc,dcb \\ \end{array}\text{.} \end{equation*}
