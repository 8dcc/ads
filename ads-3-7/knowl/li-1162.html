Item 6.4.3.9.c.
The matrices are defined on the same set \(A=\left\{a_1,a_2,\ldots ,a_n\right\}\text{.}\) Let \(c\left(a_i\right), i=1,2,\ldots ,n\) be the equivalence classes defined by \(R\) and let \(d\left(a_i\right)\) be those defined by \(S\text{.}\) Claim: \(c\left(a_i\right)\subseteq d\left(a_i\right)\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{split} a_j\in c\left(a_i\right)&\Rightarrow a_i r a_j\\ &\Rightarrow R_{ij}=1 \Rightarrow S_{ij}=1\\ &\Rightarrow a_i s a_j\\ & \Rightarrow a_j \in d\left(a_i\right)\\ \end{split} \end{equation*}
