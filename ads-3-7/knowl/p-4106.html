To determine whether H, a subset of group \([G;*]\text{,}\) is a subgroup, it is sufficient to prove:

  1. H is closed under \(*\text{;}\) that is, \(a, b \in H \Rightarrow a * b \in H\text{;}\)

  2. H contains the identity element for \(*\text{;}\) and

  3. H contains the inverse of each of its elements; that is, \(a\in H \Rightarrow a^{-1}\in H\text{.}\)
