Let's consider a somewhat larger case. We start by selecting a modulus that can be factored into a product of relatively prime integers: \(n=21,600=2^5 3^3 5^2\text{.}\) In this case the factors are \(2^5=32\text{,}\) \(3^3=27\text{,}\) and \(5^2=25\text{.}\) They need not be powers of primes, but it is easy to break the factors into this form to assure relatively prime numbers. To add in \(\mathbb{Z}_n\text{,}\) we need \(\left\lceil \log _2n\right\rceil =15\) time units. Let \(G=\mathbb{Z}_{32}\times \mathbb{Z}_{27}\times \mathbb{Z}_{25}\text{.}\) The CRT gives us an isomorphism between \(\mathbb{Z}_{21600}\) and \(G\text{.}\) The basic idea behind the fast adder, illustrated in FigureÂ 15.1.16, is to make use of this isomorphism.
