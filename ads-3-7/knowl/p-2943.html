If \(G(A;z) = (1 + z)^3\) , then we expand \((1 + z)^3\) to \(1 + 3z + 3z^2 + z^{3}\) . Therefore \(A(0) = 1\text{,}\) \(A(1) = 3\) \(A(2)= 3\text{,}\) \(A(3) = 1\text{,}\) and, since there are no higher-powered terms, \(A(n) = 0\text{,}\) \(n \geq 4\text{.}\) A more concise way of describing \(A\) is \(A(k) = \binom{3}{k} \text{,}\) since \(\binom{n}{k} \) is interpreted as 0 of \(k > n\text{.}\)
