Item 4.

For those familiar with vector calculus, we are expressing the vector \(x = a_1 \boldsymbol{\hat{\textbf{i}}}+ a_2 \boldsymbol{\hat{\textbf{j}}} + a_3 \boldsymbol{\hat{\textbf{k}}} \in \mathbb{R}^3\) as \(\left(a_1,a_2,a_3\right)\text{.}\) This allows us to discuss vectors in \(\mathbb{R}^n\) in much simpler notation.
