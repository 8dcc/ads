From Example 12.3.8 we found that if we choose \(a_1=1\) and \(a_2=1\text{,}\) then the two vectors \((4, 1)\) and \((1,3)\) generate the vector \((5, 4)\text{.}\) Another geometric interpretation of this problem is that the coordinates of the position \((5, 4)\) with respect to the \(x'y'\) axes of Figure 12.3.10 is \((1, 1)\text{.}\) In other words, a position in the plane has the name \((5, 4)\) in reference to the \(xy\)-axes and the same position has the name \((1, 1)\) in reference to the \(x'y'\) axes.
