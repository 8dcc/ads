Let \(A = B = \{1,2, 3\}\) and let \(g = \{(1, 2), (2, 3), (2, 1),(3, 2)\}\text{.}\) We note that \(g\) acts on all of \(A\text{.}\) However, \(g\) is still not a function since \((2, 3) \in g\) and \((2, 1) \in g\) and the condition on each domain being related to exactly one element of the codomain is violated.
