In this proof, the symbols \(s_i\) and \(t_i\) stand for transpositions and \(p\text{,}\) \(q\) are even nonnegative integers. If \(f, g \in A_n\text{,}\) we can write the two permutations as products of even numbers of transpositions, \(f=s_1s_2\cdots s_p\) and \(g=t_1t_2\cdots t_q\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} f\circ g=s_1s_2\cdots s_pt_1t_2\cdots t_q \end{equation*}

Since \(p+q\) is even, \(f\circ g \in A_n\text{,}\) and \(A_n\) is closed with respect to function composition. With this, we have proven that \(A_n\) is a subgroup of \(S_n\) by TheoremÂ 11.5.5.
