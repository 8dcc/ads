The direct product of the positive real numbers with the integers modulo 4, \(\mathbb{R}^+ \times \mathbb{Z}_4\) is an infinite group since one of its factors is infinite. The operations on the factors are multiplication and modular addition, so we will select the neutral symbol \(\diamond\) for the operation on \(\mathbb{R}^+ \times \mathbb{Z}_4\text{.}\) If \(a = (4, 3)\) and \(b = (0.5, 2)\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c} a \diamond b = (4, 3) \diamond (0.5, 2) = \left(4 \cdot 0.5, 3 +_4 2\right) = (2, 1)\\ b^2 = b \diamond b = (0.5, 2) \diamond (0.5, 2) = (0.25, 0)\\ a^{-1} = \left(4^{-1} , -3\right) = (0.25, 1)\\ b^{-1} = \left(0.5^{-1} , -2\right) = (2, 2) \end{array} \end{equation*}
