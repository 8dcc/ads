We can replace any vector in a basis is with a nonzero multiple of that vector. Simply for aesthetic reasons, we will multiply the second vector that generates \(E_2\) by 2. Therefore, the eigenspace \(E_2\) is a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^3\) with basis \(\left\{\left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{array} \right),\left( \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ \end{array} \right)\right\}\) and so \(\dim E_2 = 2\text{.}\)
