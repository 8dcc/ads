Consider the group of real numbers, \([\mathbb{R}; +]\text{,}\) and its subgroup of integers, \(\mathbb{Z}\text{.}\) Every element of \(\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}\) has the same cardinality as \(\mathbb{Z}\text{.}\) Let \(s, t\in \mathbb{R}\text{.}\) \(s\in t+\mathbb{Z}\) if \(s\) can be written \(t+n\) for some \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\text{.}\) Hence \(s\) and \(t\) belong to the same coset if they differ by an integer.
