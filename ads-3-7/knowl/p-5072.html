The expression \(x_1 \cdot x_2\) represents the situation in which a series of two switches appears in sequence as in Figure 13.7.4. In order for current to flow through the circuit, both switches must be ON; that is, they must both have the value 1. Similarly, a pair of parallel switches, as in Figure 13.7.5, is described algebraically by \(x_1 + x_2\text{.}\) Here, current flows through this part of the circuit as long as at least on of the switches is ON.
