Example 15.1.4. The Group of Integers is Cyclic.

The additive group of integers, \([\mathbb{Z}; +]\text{,}\) is cyclic:

\begin{equation*} \mathbb{Z} = \langle 1 \rangle = \{n\cdot 1 |n \in \mathbb{Z}\} \end{equation*}

This observation does not mean that every integer is the product of an integer times 1. It means that

\begin{equation*} \mathbb{Z} = \{0\} \cup \{\overbrace{1+1+\cdots +1}^{n \textrm{ terms}} \mid n \in \mathbb{P} \} \cup \{\overbrace{(-1)+(-1)+\cdots +(-1)}^{n \textrm{ terms}} \mid n \in \mathbb{P}\} \end{equation*}
