Item 3.5.4.3.b.

Direct proof:

  1. \((p\to q)\land (r\to s)\)

  2. \(p\to q\)

  3. \((p\to t)\land (s\to u)\)

  4. \(q\to t\)

  5. \(p\to t\)

  6. \(r\to s\)

  7. \(s\to u\)

  8. \(r\to u\)

  9. \(p\to r\)

  10. \(p\to u\)

  11. \(p\to (t\land u)\) Use \((x\to y)\land (x\to z)\Leftrightarrow x\to (y\land z)\)

  12. \(\neg (t\land u)\to \neg p\)

  13. \(\neg (t\land u)\)

  14. \(\neg p\) \(\quad \square\)

Indirect proof:

  1. \(p\)

  2. \(p\to q\)

  3. \(q\)

  4. \(q\to t\)

  5. \(t\)

  6. \(\neg (t\land u)\)

  7. \(\neg t\lor \neg u\)

  8. \(\neg u\)

  9. \(s\to u\)

  10. \(\neg s\)

  11. \(r\to s\)

  12. \(\neg r\)

  13. \(p\to r\)

  14. \(r\)

  15. \(0\) \(\quad \square\)
