Another universal concept that applies to the theory of rings is that of a subsystem. A subring of a ring \([R; +, \cdot ]\) is any nonempty subset \(S\) of \(R\) that is a ring under the operations of \(R\text{.}\) First, for \(S\) to be a subring of the ring \(R\text{,}\) \(S\) must be a subgroup of the group \([R; +]\text{.}\) Also, \(S\) must be closed under \(\cdot\text{,}\) satisfy the associative law under \(\cdot\text{,}\) and satisfy the distributive laws.
