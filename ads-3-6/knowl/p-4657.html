Prove that if \(P\) is an \(n\times n\) matrix and \(D\) is a diagonal matrix with diagonal entries \(d_1\text{,}\) \(d_2,\ldots,\) \(d_n\text{,}\) then \(P D\) is the matrix obtained from \(P\text{,}\) by multiplying column \(i\) of \(P\) by \(d_i\text{,}\) \(i = 1, 2, \ldots, n\text{.}\)
