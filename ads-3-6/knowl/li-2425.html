Item 15.3.3:2.

The identity for the group is \(i\text{.}\) If \(g\) is any one of the permutations on \(A\) and \(x\in A\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{lr} (g\circ i)(x) = g(i(x)) = g(x) &(i\circ g)(x) = i(g(x)) = g(x)\\ \end{array} \end{equation*}

Therefore \(g\circ i = i\circ g=g\text{.}\)
