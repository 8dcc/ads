A finite-state machine can be designed to add positive integers of any size. Given two integers in binary form, \(a=a_na_{n-1} \cdots a_1a_0\) and \(b=b_n b_{n-1}\cdots b_1b_0\text{,}\) the machine take as its input sequence the corresponding bits of \(a\) and \(b\) reading from right to left with a “parity bit” added

\begin{equation*} a_0b_0\left(a_0+_2b_0\right) ,a_1b_1\left(a_1+_2b_1\right) \ldots , a_nb_n\left(a_n+_2b_n\right),111 \end{equation*}

Notice the special input 111 at the end. All possible inputs except the last one must even parity (contain an even number of ones). The output sequence is the sum of \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) starting with the units digit, and comes from the set \(\{0,1,\lambda \}\text{.}\) The transition diagram for this machine appears in Figure 14.3.10.
