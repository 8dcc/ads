Exercise 7.

We noted in Chapter 5 that since matrix algebra is not commutative under multiplication, certain difficulties arise. Let \(A=\left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \\ \end{array} \right)\) and \(B=\left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 \\ \end{array} \right)\text{.}\)

  1. Compute \(e^A\text{,}\) \(e^B\text{,}\) and \(e^{A+B}\text{.}\) Compare \(e^A e^{B}\text{,}\) \(e^B e^A\) and \(e^{A+B}\) .

  2. Show that if \(\pmb{0}\) is the \(2\times 2\) zero matrix, then \(e^{\pmb{0}}= I\text{.}\)

  3. Prove that if \(A\) and \(B\) are two matrices that do commute, then \(e^{A+B}=e^Ae^B\text{,}\) thereby proving that \(e^A\) and \(e^B\) commute.

  4. Prove that for any matrix \(A\text{,}\) \(\left(e^A\right)^{-1}= e^{-A}\text{.}\)
