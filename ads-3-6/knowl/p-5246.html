Consider the parity checker. The following table summarizes the effect on the parity checker of strings in \(B^1\) and \(B^2\text{.}\) The row labeled “Even” contains the final state and final output as a result of each input string in \(B^1\) and \(B^2\) when the machine starts in the even state. Similarly, the row labeled “Odd” contains the same information for input sequences when the machine starts in the odd state.

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|cccccc} \textrm{ Input} \textrm{ String} & 0 & 1 & 00 & 01 & 10 & 11 \\ \hline \textrm{ Even} & (\textrm{ Even},0) & (\textrm{ Odd},1) & (\textrm{ Even},0) & (\textrm{ Odd},1) & (\textrm{ Odd},1) & (\textrm{ Even},0) \\ \textrm{ Odd} & (\textrm{ Odd},1) & (\textrm{ Even},1) & (\textrm{ Odd},1) & (\textrm{ Even},1) & (\textrm{ Even},0) & (\textrm{ Odd},1) \\ \textrm{ Same} \textrm{ Effect} \textrm{ as} & & & 0 & 1 & 1 & 0 \\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
