Let \(A = \{0, 1,2,3\}\text{,}\) and let

\begin{equation*} r = \{(0, 0), (0, 3), (1, 2), (2, 1), (3, 2), (2, 0)\} \end{equation*}

In representing this relation as a graph, elements of \(A\) are called the vertices of the graph. They are typically represented by labeled points or small circles. We connect vertex \(a\) to vertex \(b\) with an arrow, called an edge, going from vertex \(a\) to vertex \(b\) if and only if \(a r b\text{.}\) This type of graph of a relation \(r\) is called a directed graph or digraph. Figure 6.2.1 is a digraph for \(r\text{.}\) Notice that since 0 is related to itself, we draw a “self-loop” at 0.
