A ring \([R; + , \cdot ]\) that has a multiplicative identity is called a ring with unity. The multiplicative identity itself is called the unity of the ring. More formally, if there exists an element \(1 \in R\text{,}\) such that for all \(x \in R\text{,}\) \(x\cdot 1 = 1\cdot x = x\text{,}\) then \(R\) is called a ring with unity.
