On notation. If two or more consecutive factors in a direct product are identical, it is common to combine them using exponential notation. For example, \(\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{R}\) can be written \(\mathbb{Z}^2 \times \mathbb{R}\text{,}\) and \(\mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}\) can be written \(\mathbb{R}^4\text{.}\) This is purely a notational convenience; no exponentiation is really taking place.
