Group \(G\) is cyclic if there exists \(a \in G\) such that the cyclic subgroup generated by \(a\text{,}\) \(\langle a \rangle\text{,}\) equals all of \(G\text{.}\) That is, \(G = \{n a |n \in \mathbb{Z}\}\text{,}\) in which case \(a\) is called a generator of \(G\text{.}\) The reader should note that additive notation is used for \(G\text{.}\)
