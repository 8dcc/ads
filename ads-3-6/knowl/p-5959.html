Let \([F; +, \cdot]\) be a field and let \(f(x) \in F[x]\) be a nonconstant polynomial. \(f(x)\) is reducible over \(F\) if and only if it cannot be factored as a product of two nonconstant polynomials in \(F[x]\text{.}\) A polynomial is irreducible over \(F\) if it is not reducible over \(F\text{.}\)
