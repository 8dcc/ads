If \(\lvert A_1 \rvert = \lvert A_2 \rvert\text{,}\) then the monoids \(A_1^*\) and \(A_2^*\) are isomorphic. An isomorphism can be defined using any bijection \(f:A_1\to A_2\text{.}\) If \(a=a_1a_2\cdots a_n \in A_1^*\text{,}\) \(f^*(a)=(f(a_1)f(a_2)\cdots f(a_n))\) defines a bijection from \(A_1^*\) into \(A_2^*\text{.}\) We will leave it to the reader to prove that for all \(a,b,\in A_1^*,f^*(a+b)=f^*(a)+f^*(b)\text{.}\)
