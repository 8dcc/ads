Theorem2.1.6
If \(A\) is a finite set, then \(|\mathcal{P}(A) | = 2^{|A|}\)
Proof
Proof: Consider how we might determine any \(B \in \mathcal{P}(A)\), where \( |A|=n\). For each element \(x \in A\) there are two choices, either \(x \in B\) or \(x \notin B\). Since there are \(n\) elements of \(A\) we have, by the rule of products, \[\underset{n\backslash \textrm{factors}}{\underline{2\cdot 2\backslash \cdot \backslash \cdots \cdot 2}}\right\backslash =\left\backslash 2^n\] different subsets of A. Therefore, \(\textscr{P}(A)= 2^{n}\);
