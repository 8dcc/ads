Theorem 12.3.15. The fundamental property of a basis.

If \(\left\{\vec{x}_1,\vec{x}_2, \ldots ,\vec{x}_n\right\}\) is a basis for a vector space V over \(\mathbb{R}\text{,}\) then any vector \(y \in V\) can be uniquely expressed as a linear combination of the \(\vec{x}_i\textrm{'s}\text{.}\)
