When we look at groups like \(\mathbb{Z}_2{}^4\) from a point of view, we refer to these sets as metric spaces or simply spaces. In the case of \(\mathbb{Z}_2{}^4\text{,}\) there are just \(2^4=16\) points in the space and the maximum distance between the points is \(4\text{.}\) More generally \(\mathbb{Z}_2{}^n\) has \(2^n\) points and the maximum distance between points in that space is \(n\text{.}\) Looking at the group \(\mathbb{Z}_2{}^n\) from this geometric point of view is essentially the same as the \(n\)-cubeÂ 9.4.17 we considered in discussing Hamiltonian graphs. In this section we will use \(n\)-tuples such as \((1,1,0,1)\) interchangeably with strings of bits such as \(1101\text{.}\)
