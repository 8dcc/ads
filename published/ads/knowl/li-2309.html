Item 13.1.7.d.
The least upper bound doesn’t exist. Notice that the union of \(A\) and \(B\) isn’t in \(\mathcal{P}_0\text{.}\) One of the two sets \(\{1,2,3,5,6,7\}\) and \(\{1,2,3,6,7,9\}\) is contained within every upper bound of \(A\) and \(B\) but neither is contained within the other.
