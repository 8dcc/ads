If
\begin{equation*} A =\left( \begin{array}{cc} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \\ \end{array} \right), X =\left( \begin{array}{c} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ \end{array} \right)\textrm{, and }B =\left( \begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 1 \\ \end{array} \right), \end{equation*}
show that \(A X =B\) is a way of expressing the system \(\begin{array}{c}2x_1 + x_2 = 3\\ x_1 - x_2= 1\\ \end{array}\) using matrices.
