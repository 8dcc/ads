The equation \(x + x = x \cdot x\text{,}\) or \(2x=x^2\text{,}\) makes sense in both rings. However, this equation has a nonzero solution, \(x = 2\text{,}\) in \(2\mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) but does not have a nonzero solution in \(3\mathbb{Z}\text{.}\) Thus we have an equation solvable in one ring that cannot be solved in the other, so they cannot be isomorphic.
