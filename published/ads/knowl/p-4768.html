Let \(A\) be an \(n\times n\) matrix over \(\mathbb{R}\text{.}\) \(\lambda\) is an eigenvalue of \(A\) if for some nonzero column vector \(\vec{x}\in \mathbb{R}^n\) we have \(A \vec{x} = \lambda \vec{x}\text{.}\) \(\vec{x}\) is called an eigenvector corresponding to the eigenvalue \(\lambda\text{.}\)
