We can describe \(\mathcal{D}_n\) in terms of symmetries of a regular \(n\)-gon (\(n = 3\text{:}\) equilateral triangle, \(n = 4\text{:}\) square, \(n = 5\text{:}\) regular pentagon,\(\ldots \)). Here we will only concentrate on the case of \(\mathcal{D}_4\text{.}\) If a square is fixed in space, there are several motions of the square that will, at the end of the motion, not change the apparent position of the square.
