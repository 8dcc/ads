  1. In the ring \([\mathbb{Z}_8;+_8,\times_8]\text{,}\) the numbers 4 and 2 are zero divisors since \(4 \times_8 2 =0\text{.}\) In addition, 6 is a zero divisor because \(6\times_8 4 = 0\text{.}\)
  2. In the ring \(\left[M_{2\times 2}(\mathbb{R}); +, \cdot \right]\) the matrices \(A=\left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \\ \end{array} \right)\) and \(B=\left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \\ \end{array} \right)\) are zero divisors since \(A B = 0\text{.}\)
  3. \([\mathbb{Z}; +, \cdot]\) has no zero divisors.
