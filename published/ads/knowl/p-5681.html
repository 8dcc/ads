Consider the trigonometric function sine. Given that \(\sin (x+2\pi k) = \sin x\) for all \(x\in \mathbb{R}\) and \(k\in \mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) show how the distinguished representatives of \(\mathbb{R}/\langle 2\pi \rangle\) can be useful in developing an algorithm for calculating the sine of a number.
