Suppose that \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(a'\text{,}\) \(b'\text{.}\) are two choices for representatives of cosets \(C\) and \(D\text{.}\) That is to say that \(a, a' \in C\text{,}\) \(b, b' \in D\text{.}\) We will show that \(a*b\) and \(a'*b'\) are representatives of the same coset. TheoremÂ 15.2.61 implies that \(C = a*H\) and \(D = b*H\text{,}\) thus we have \(a' \in a*H\) and \(b' \in b*H\text{.}\) Then there exists \(h_1, h_2 \in H\) such that \(a' = a*h_1\) and \(b' = b*h_2\) and so
\begin{equation*} a'*b' = (a*h_1)*(b*h_2) = (a*b)*(h_1*h_2) \end{equation*}
by various group properties and the assumption that \(G\) is abelian, which lets us reverse the order in which \(b\) and \(h_1\) appear in the chain of equalities. This last expression for \(a'*b'\) implies that \(a'*b' \in (a*b)*H\) since \(h_1*h_2 \in H\) because \(H\) is a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\) Thus, we get the same coset for both pairs of representatives.
