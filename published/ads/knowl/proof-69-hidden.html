Let \(K=\textrm{ker }\theta\text{.}\) We can see that \(K\) is a subgroup of \(G\) by letting \(a,b \in K\) and verify that \(a*b^{-1} \in K\) by computing \(\theta(a*b^{-1})= \theta(a)*\theta(b)^{-1} = e'*e'^{-1}=e'\text{.}\) To prove normality, we let \(g\) be any element of \(G\) and \(k \in K\text{.}\) We compute \(\theta(g*k*g^{-1})\) to verify that \(g*k*g^{-1}\in K\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{split} \theta(g*k*g^{-1}) &=\theta(g)*\theta(k)*\theta(g^{-1})\\ & =\theta(g)*\theta(k)*\theta(g)^{-1}\\ & =\theta(g)*e'*\theta(g)^{-1}\\ & =\theta(g)* \theta(g)^{-1}\\ & =e'\\ \end{split} \end{equation*}
